🎎 Cultural Notes

Understanding Japanese culture is essential for a smooth and respectful trip. Here are a few key points:

  • Bowing: Bowing is a common greeting and expression of respect. The depth of the bow indicates the level of respect.
  • Shoes: Remove your shoes when entering homes, temples, and some traditional restaurants. Slippers are often provided.
  • Chopsticks: Learn proper chopstick etiquette. Do not stick them upright in your rice bowl, as this resembles a funeral ritual.
  • Silence: Be mindful of noise levels, especially in public transportation and quiet areas like temples and gardens.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Japan. Good service is expected and included in the price.

🍜 Food Recommendations

Japanese cuisine is diverse and delicious. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Sushi: Fresh seafood on vinegared rice. Try nigiri, maki, and sashimi.
  • Ramen: Noodle soup with various toppings. Each region has its unique ramen style.
  • Tempura: Deep-fried seafood and vegetables. Enjoy it with tentsuyu dipping sauce.
  • Okonomiyaki: Savory pancake with various ingredients. A popular street food in Osaka and Hiroshima.
  • Yakitori: Grilled chicken skewers. A great snack or accompaniment to drinks.
  • Kaiseki: A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner. Experience the art of Japanese haute cuisine.

✈️ Travel Tips

  • Japan Rail Pass: If you plan to travel extensively by train, consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass. It can save you money on Shinkansen fares.
  • Pocket WiFi: Rent a pocket WiFi device to stay connected to the internet. It's essential for navigation and communication.
  • Cash: While credit cards are becoming more widely accepted, it's still a good idea to carry cash, especially for smaller establishments and temples.
  • Suica/Pasmo Card: Get a Suica or Pasmo card for easy payment on public transportation. You can also use it at vending machines and convenience stores.
  • Learn Basic Japanese: Knowing a few basic Japanese phrases will enhance your travel experience and show respect for the local culture.

🚄 Transportation Tips

  • Shinkansen (Bullet Train): The fastest and most efficient way to travel between cities. Book your tickets in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Subway: Tokyo and Kyoto have extensive subway networks. Use Google Maps or a local transportation app to navigate.
  • Buses: Buses are a common mode of transportation in Kyoto. They can be crowded, especially during rush hour.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available but can be expensive. They are useful for short distances or when traveling with luggage.
  • Walking: Japan is a very walkable country. Take advantage of walking tours to explore cities at your own pace.

🙏 Etiquette Tips

  • Public Bathrooms: Many public bathrooms have advanced features like heated seats and bidet functions.
  • Eating in Public: It's generally acceptable to eat on trains and in parks, but avoid eating while walking.
  • Gift Giving: When giving a gift, present it with both hands and offer a humble remark.
  • Business Cards: Business cards are exchanged with both hands. Treat them with respect and don't write on them immediately.
  • Facial Expressions: Be mindful of your facial expressions. Avoid excessive eye contact, which can be seen as confrontational.
