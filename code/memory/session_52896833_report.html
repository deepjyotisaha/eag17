EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Asian Paints Limited is the largest paint manufacturer in India and ranks among the top decorative coatings companies globally. The company was established in 1942 and has since built a strong reputation for its quality products and customer-centric approach. This report provides a comprehensive overview of Asian Paints, including its company overview, financial performance, competitive landscape, market trends, and SWOT analysis.

COMPANY OVERVIEW

Asian Paints Limited, established in 1942, is India's leading paint and decor company and ranks among the top 10 Decorative Coating companies globally. The company manufactures and distributes a wide range of paints, coatings, home-related products, wall coverings, waterproofing solutions, adhesives, and painting equipment [1].

The company operates in two major segments:

  1. Decorative Business: Offers a wide range of products and services along with painting services. Contributes 88.2% of revenue.
  2. Home Décor Business: Provides a complete one-stop home décor solution to customers.

Asian Paints has a significant global presence, serving customers in over 60 countries with 27 in-house paint manufacturing facilities worldwide [1].

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE

Asian Paints' financial performance can be summarized as follows:

  • Revenue: In FY23-24, the company reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹34,785 crore.
  • Net Sales (Standalone): Q4 FY24-25 Net Sales stood at ₹7,157.1 Crores (-5.0%).
  • Net Profit (Standalone): Q4 FY24-25 Net Profit stood at ₹694.3 Crores (-42.6%).

The company focuses on volume growth and margin improvement through strategic pricing and cost optimization [2].

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

Key competitors of Asian Paints include:

  • Berger Paints
  • Kansai Nerolac Paints
  • Akzo Nobel India
  • Nippon Paint

Asian Paints holds a market share of approximately 59% in India, making it the market leader. Berger Paints is the second-largest player with a market share of around 18% [3].

MARKET TRENDS & INDUSTRY ANALYSIS

The Asia-Pacific paints and coatings market is experiencing growth, with a projected market size of USD 81.22 billion in 2025, expected to reach USD 103.66 billion by 2030, at a CAGR of greater than 5% [4].

Key trends include:

  • Increasing environmental consciousness and the development of eco-friendly paint and coating solutions with lower VOC emissions.
  • Robust manufacturing sector driving demand for industrial coatings.
  • Infrastructure development initiatives creating new opportunities for protective and architectural coatings.
