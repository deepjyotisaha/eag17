🚀 Recent and Upcoming Launches

Mahindra Bolero Neo Plus

The Bolero Neo Plus is an extended version of the Bolero Neo, likely offered with 7 and 9-seat configurations. It also features an ambulance variant with a 4-seat layout and a patient bed. The SUV is powered by a 2.2L mHawk diesel engine with a 6-speed manual gearbox, producing 130PS and 300Nm of torque. Key features include body-colored pillars and a tailgate-mounted spare tire. [1]

5-door Mahindra Thar

The 5-door Mahindra Thar is expected to be launched by the end of 2023 or early 2024. It features a 300mm longer wheelbase than the 3-door model and is based on the Scorpio-N's ladder-frame chassis. The SUV will have an extra door for middle-row passengers and is expected to share powertrains with the Scorpio N, including a 200bhp 2.0L turbo petrol and a 172bhp 2.2L turbo diesel engine. [2]

New-gen Mahindra Bolero

The next-gen Bolero is expected in 2024 and is based on the new ladder-frame chassis that underpins the Thar and Scorpio-N. It is likely to retain the original look but with new design elements and body panels. The cabin will receive maximum changes with better fit & finish levels and material quality. It could also get modern features like a touchscreen infotainment system and automatic AC. [1]

New Mahindra XUV500

The XUV500 nameplate will return with significant changes, positioned between the XUV300 and XUV700. It is expected to feature ADAS tech and other interior upgrades. The new XUV500 is expected to carry forward the same engine from the XUV700, but in a detuned state to make less power. [2]

Mahindra XUV300 Facelift

Launched in February 2024, the XUV300 Facelift features a redesigned front and rear fascia, a larger touchscreen infotainment system, and new connectivity capabilities. It offers multiple engine options, including a 1.2 L MPI Turbo-Petrol, 1.2 L TGDI Petrol, and 1.5 L Turbo Diesel engines. A panoramic sunroof is also included. [3]

Mahindra XUV300 EV

Expected in the second half of 2024, the XUV300 EV will likely get a 35-kWh battery pack with an expected range of 400+ kilometers on a single charge. It will be placed below the XUV400 and offered in the FWD variant initially. [3]

Mahindra Thar Armada

Also expected in the second half of 2024, the Thar Armada is based on the ladder frame chassis and features a similar design language to the three-door Thar. It will have a redesigned dashboard with a larger touchscreen infotainment system and an electric sunroof. Engine options include a 2.0 L MStallion Turbo-Petrol and a 2.2 L MHawk Diesel. [3]

Mahindra XUV.e8

Expected at the end of 2024, the XUV.e8 is the all-electric version of the XUV700. The electric SUV is expected to be offered with two battery pack options, ranging from 60 kWh to 80 kWh, along with the availability of a dual-motor all-wheel drivetrain. [2]

Mahindra XUV400 Facelift

Expected in the second half of 2024, this updated EV is expected to come with an improved range while retaining its existing 34.5 kWh and 39.4 kWh battery pack options that could offer better range. [2]
